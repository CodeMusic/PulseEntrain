# Field Meditation Mode — Navigating the Binaural Field

> *A guide to the space behind the circle.*
> Grounded in the science of the frequency-following response — shaped for wonder.

There is a glowing circle at the center of the screen. It looks like one thing.
It is really a **window into a space** — and Field Meditation Mode is how you move
through it. Not with menus and sliders, but by **looking** and **feeling**.

---

## The field

Picture every possible entrainment as a room you can stand inside. It has three
directions. Give the room coordinates and each point becomes a distinct *tuning* —
a particular texture of sound and light, and (the wager of this whole app) a
particular texture of attention.

### X — the carrier · *the voice*

The **carrier** is the underlying tone — its pitch, from a low ~80 Hz hum to a
bright ~500 Hz shimmer. On its own the carrier doesn't entrain anything; it's the
*canvas* the beat is painted on, the color of the light you're standing in. In the
app it literally sets the circle's colour: walk left for deep indigo lows, right
for luminous highs.

### Y — the binaural beat · *the pulse*

Play two tones, one in each ear, a few hertz apart — say **200 Hz** left and
**210 Hz** right — and you don't hear two notes. You hear a third thing that exists
in neither ear: a slow **10 Hz beat**, assembled inside your head. That beat lands
in a brain-wave band:

| Band | Beat | Loosely | 
|---|---|---|
| Delta | 0.5–4 Hz | deep rest |
| Theta | 4–8 Hz | reverie, meditation |
| Alpha | 8–13 Hz | calm, open focus |
| Beta | 13–30 Hz | alert, engaged |
| Gamma | 30 Hz+ | bright, binding |

The idea underneath is the **frequency-following response (FFR)** — the tendency of
neural rhythms to fall into step with a steady external rhythm. The auditory FFR is
real and well documented; whether it reliably *shifts a state of mind* is
suggestive, not settled. Treat a coordinate as a **doorway**, not a lever.

### Z — the biphotic (binocular) beat · *the shimmer*

Now the visual cousin. Flash each eye at a **slightly different rate** and the
visual system perceives a beat at the difference — a slow shimmer present in
neither eye alone, only in their meeting. It's studied under names like *binocular
beats*, *dichoptic flicker*, and *SSVEP intermodulation*; more experimental than
the binaural beat, and honestly newer ground. In the field it's the **biphotic
beat**, and it opens when you **tilt your head** — one eye's light slowing against
the other until a third rhythm appears between them.

---

## Coordinates are tunings · pathways are journeys

A single point `(carrier, binaural, biphotic)` is a **tuning** — a place to rest.

A **path** through the space is a **journey**. As you glide the frequencies, the
following response follows, and the felt state travels with it — restless → still,
scattered → gathered, awake → dreaming and back. Meditation here isn't only *sit
and wait*. It's *find your coordinate, then let the field carry you* — and,
sometimes, *trace a path* from the state you're in toward the one you're reaching
for.

## Looking and feeling

You navigate two ways at once — the outer hand and the inner gaze.

**Feel — touch.** A ROLI **Lightpad Block** becomes the floor of the space under
your fingertip:

- left ↔ right → the **carrier**
- up ↕ down → the **binaural beat** (sound *and* the light's flash rate together)
- press deeper → the whole field **brightens and intensifies**

> *On a phone with no Lightpad, the screen itself becomes the pad — coming soon.*

**Look — gaze.** With the **Lumenate Nova** resting on your brow, its accelerometer
reads where you look. Press and hold, then move your head:

- **pitch** (look up / down) → the **beat and flash rate** (down slows toward a
  single deep pulse; up quickens toward the bright end)
- **roll** (tilt left / right) → the **biphotic shimmer**, slowing the light on the
  side you lean toward while the other holds

> *On a phone with no Nova, the device's own accelerometer stands in — coming soon.*

So you don't just *look* around the field. You **feel** around it — two senses
reaching into the same space, sound and light bending together under hand and head.

---

## Press · wander · release

The gesture is simple, and it's the whole practice:

1. **Press** the block to step into the editable field — the light swells to show
   you've entered.
2. **Wander** — drift your finger, or move your gaze, and watch the tuning change.
   Look *and* feel; the two blend.
3. **Release.** The moment you lift, the tuning **locks in place**. Nothing moves.

And there, at the coordinate you found, you're left to **absorb · reflect · emit** —
to receive the state, to notice it, and to carry it back out with you.

---

## Grounded, and honest

This is a wellness and contemplative tool, **not a medical device** and not a
treatment for any condition. The evidence for brain-wave entrainment is real but
mixed — hold it lightly, and let the *experience* be the point.

⚠️ **Photosensitivity.** The light flickers. Flashing light can trigger seizures in
people with photosensitive epilepsy. Don't use the Nova if you (or anyone who can
see it) may be photosensitive, and stop immediately if you feel unwell.

Binaural and biphotic beats both need the two sides kept separate — **stereo
headphones** for the ears, and the Nova's per-eye light for the eyes.

*Carrier, binaural beat, biphotic beat — three simple differences, layered. From
them, a space; through the space, a path; along the path, maybe, a more harmonized
and resonant state of mind.* ♥
